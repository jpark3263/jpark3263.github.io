‚ú®About Me

I'm a PhD student at Georgia Tech, the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering advised by Prof. Saibal Mukhopadhyay, the Gigascale Reliable Energy-Efficient Nanosystem Lab. I graduated with a BS in Electronic and Electrical Engineering from the Sungkyunkwan University in Feb 2021, graduated with a MS in Electrical Engineering from the KAIST in Feb 2023. After completing my master's degree, I joined Samsung Electronics as an RFIC design engineer, gaining hands-on experience in developing RF front-end circuits. Currently, I am expanding my expertise to system level research in Georgia Tech GREEN Lab.

My research is focuses on studying Low-power and variation tolerant VLSI systems. Specifically, I am interested in how can we developing computationally efficient AI/ML methods coupling architecture, circuit, and device innovations. As a result, my past work has spanned

  • Dynamic configuration of RF front-end based on feature/task-driven feedback, developing a algorithms to predict environmental changes and preemptively adjust RF front-end parameters to achieve energy-efficient real-time adaptability.
  • Energy-Efficient RF Processing Units, algorithm-architecture co-design techniques to reduce memory and computation demands of learning algorithms during training and inference in RF-centric domain.

In my free time, I enjoy traveling, cooking (and eating), running, and watching basketball (NBA and college).
