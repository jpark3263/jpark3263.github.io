About Me

I'm a PhD student at the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering and the Gigascale Reliable Energy-Efficient Nanosystem Lab at Georgia Tech. I am fortunate to be advised by Saibal Mukhopadhyay. I graduated with a BS in Electronic and Electrical Engineering from the Sungkyunkwan University in Feb 2021, graduated with a MS in Electrical Engineering from the KAIST in Feb 2023.

My research is focuses on studying energy-efficient design from both an empirical and theoretical perspective. Specifically, I am interested in how can we developing computationally efficient AI/ML methods coupling architecture, circuit, and device innovations. As a result, my past work has spanned

  • Processing-in-memory, developing a unique in-memory and programmable computing platform to accelerate training and inference of deep learning networks.
  • Energy-efficient training and inference, algorithm-architecture co-design techniques to reduce memory and computation demands of learning algorithms during training and inference.
  • Exploring post-CMOS devices for Learning, application of emerging devices such as Resistive devices (RRAM), Ferroelectric FETs (FeFETs), and Tunneling transistors for machine learning platforms.

In my free time, I enjoy traveling, cooking (and eating), running, and watching basketball (NBA and college).
