About Me

I'm a PhD student at the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering and the Gigascale Reliable Energy-Efficient Nanosystem Lab at Georgia Tech advised by Saibal Mukhopadhyay. I graduated with a BS in Electronic and Electrical Engineering from the Sungkyunkwan University in Feb 2021, graduated with a MS in Electrical Engineering from the KAIST in Feb 2023.

My research is focuses on studying energy-efficient design from both an empirical and theoretical perspective. Specifically, I am interested in how can we developing computationally efficient AI/ML methods coupling architecture, circuit, and device innovations. As a result, my past work has spanned

  • AI-Enhanced Power Management in FMCW Radar, developing a learning algorithms to predict environmental changes and preemptively adjust RF front-end parameters to achieve real-time adaptability.
  • Energy-efficient RF Processing Units, algorithm-architecture co-design techniques to reduce memory and computation demands of learning algorithms during training and inference in RF-centric domain.

In my free time, I enjoy traveling, cooking (and eating), running, and watching basketball (NBA and college).
